# Public Source Research — FPH and HarmonyWiz

Date accessed: 2026-05-02 (UTC)

## Scope and legal boundary
Research below is used strictly for functional/pedagogical inspiration. No proprietary code, branding, text, or pixel-exact layout is copied.

## FPH (public pages)
- VSW Studio listing / references indicate four-part harmony workflow with advanced chord types and checking-oriented interaction.
- Observed functional ideas to adapt generically:
  1. Immediate correctness feedback after edits.
  2. Rule-oriented pedagogical cues instead of opaque scores.
  3. Harmonization assist layered over user-authored lines.

Primary link: https://vsw-studio.eu/fph
Additional public reference: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.vswstudio.fourpartharmony

## HarmonyWiz (public pages)
From public product page language:
- Single-line input -> multi-part harmonization.
- Analysis output (including figured-bass-oriented explanation).
- “Expert mode” style editing and track/sound management concepts.

Useful ideas to adapt generically:
1. Progressive novice -> expert workflow.
2. Keep harmonic explanations visible near generation output.
3. Provide playback/arrangement inspection loop.

Primary link: https://www.wizdommusic.com/apps/harmonywiz/

## How Harmonia 31 will differ
- Source-bounded 19th-century rule corpus with explicit traceability.
- 31-EDO / quarter-comma meantone model instead of 12-TET mainstream assumptions.
- Browser-first, open documentation, and license-audited dependency selection.
